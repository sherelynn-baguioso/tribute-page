Florence Nightingale

The Lady with the Lamp

Here's a time line of Florence Nightingale's life:

  • 1820 - Born in Florence, Italy.
  • 1837 - Felt God called to serve him and believed that nursing was her calling and divine purpose.
  • 1844 - Enrolled as a nursing student at Lutheran Hospital of Pastor Fliedner in Kaiserwerth, Germany.
  • 1850 - Returned to london and took a nursing job in a Middlesex hospital for ailing governesses. Her performance got her promoted as superintendent.
  • 1853 - Took the post as a superintendent at Institute for the Care of Sick Gentlewomen in Upper Harley Street, London.
  • 1854 - Sent to Crimea to attend to the sick and fallen soldiers, together with her assembled team of Nurses. She was then named "Lady with the Lamp" as she makes her evening rounds carrying a lamp in the dark hallways. Also, her impressive work with sanitation helped reduce infections which led to a decrease in mortality rate by two-thirds.
  • 1856 - Returned to england when Crimean War ended and met with a hero's welcome. She received an engraved brooch by the Queen, known as the "Nightingale Jewel". She was also granted a prize of $250,000 from the British Government.
  • 1858 - Contracted the "Crimean Fever" and made her homebound and bedridden. She still continued to work from her bed, conducted interviews with politicians and distinguished guests, and remained phenomenally productive in social reform.
  • 1860 - Funded the establishment of St. Thomas' Hospital and the Nightingale Training School for Nurses.
  • 1883 - Awarded the Royal Red Cross by Queen Victoria.
  • 1904 - Appointed a Lady of Grace of the Order of St. John.
  • 1908 - Conferred the merit of honor by King Edward VII.
  • 1910 - Received a congratulatory message from King George on her 90th birthday and died on 13th of Augus.
